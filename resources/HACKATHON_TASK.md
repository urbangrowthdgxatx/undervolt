# Hackathon Task: Urban Growth & Infrastructure Intelligence

**Prize:** $1,000 Cash

## The Dataset
Issued Construction Permits (Austin) - 2M+ rows of text

## The Problem
A city changes faster than its official census data. A building permit is just a piece of digital paperwork, but hidden inside the messy text description is the real story of the city:
- Where are the EV chargers going?
- Who is building dense housing?
- Are we adding enough power capacity?

City planners and developers are flying blind because this critical data is locked in unstructured text fields.

## The Goal
Build a system that unlocks unstructured permit data to reveal the city's physical future or economic trends.

## Suggested Directions

### 1. Hidden Trend Discovery
Extract non-standard entities (e.g., "ADUs") to map trends not found in official columns.

### 2. The Development Advisor
A RAG agent that helps builders price projects by visualizing comparable historical permits.

### 3. Economic Forecasting
Predict neighborhood gentrification speed based on renovation permit valuations.

## Example Projects

### Hidden Infrastructure Mapper
An agent that extracts non-standard entities (EV Chargers, ADUs, Bitcoin Mines) from text descriptions to show trends the city doesn't officially track.

### The Development Advisor
A RAG agent that helps builders price projects by finding and visualizing similar historical permits (e.g., "Show me the cost trend for pools in East Austin vs. West Austin").
